House Specials

The Roadwarden’s Roast

A generous cut of slow‑roasted beef glazed with garlic and herbs, served with hearth‑roasted potatoes and root vegetables.

18 crwn.

Hearthkeeper's Feast

Fire‑roasted meats and seasonal vegetables arranged for sharing, cooked low and steady to sustain travellers bound for long roads.

17 crwn.

The Marchlands Platter

A rotating selection of tavern favourites drawn from local fare — roast cuts, hand pies, and vegetables — served as the house sees fit.

16 crwn.

Starters

Traveller's Board

Warm bread, hand pies, pickled vegetables, and small savoury bites, served for sharing or quiet arrival by the hearth.

8 crwn.

Hearth Soup of the Day

A daily broth prepared from available vegetables and bones, simmered slowly and served with coarse bread and butter.

6 crwn.

Market Hand Pies

Two baked pies filled with meat or spiced vegetables, easily eaten by the road‑weary and prepared fresh each morning.

7 crwn.

Mains

Hearth-Roasted Beef

A thick slice of open‑flame beef, basted with herbs and served alongside potatoes cooked in rendered fat.

14 crwn.

Skillet-Seared Sausages

House sausages browned over the hearth, served with carrots, onions, and a ladle of savoury gravy.

17 crwn.

Braised Root Stew

A slow‑simmered stew of seasonal root vegetables and herbs, thickened with barley and served steaming hot.

11 crwn. (V)

Sides

Buttered Greens

Wilted greens dressed simply with butter and cracked pepper, served warm as a comforting hearthside accompaniment.

3 crwn.

Hearthside Potatoes

Hearth‑roasted potatoes crisped in rendered fat and finished with herbs and coarse salt.

4 crwn.

Glazed Carrots & Roots

Carrots and seasonal root vegetables slowly cooked in honey and butter until tender and lightly caramelised.

4 crwn.

Desserts

Honeyed Berry Cake

Golden sponge layered with soft cream, soaked in warm honey and topped with forest berries.

6 crwn.

Spiced Hearth Tart

A small baked tart filled with spiced fruit and nuts, served warm from the oven.

5 crwn.

Sweet Bread & Cream

Fresh‑baked sweet bread drizzled with honey and served with lightly whipped cream.

4 crwn.

Drinks

Wayfarer's Pour

House‑brewed ale, mulled wine, or spiced broth, served hot or cold depending on season.

4 crwn.

Marchlands Red

A deep, warming red wine carried along southern trade routes and traditionally served with a clove of raw garlic — a Marchlands custom believed to ward illness and steady travellers before the road ahead.

5 crwn.

Hearthbrew Ale

Dark, malty ale brewed for steady drinking by firelight.
