The Wayfarer’s Code

A small set of customs observed in every Wayfarer’s Rest across the Marchlands. These principles guide how each tavern is run, ensuring safety, fairness, and quiet refuge for all who travel the long roads.

The Tavern Code

Though each Wayfarer’s Rest reflects the land and people around it, all are bound by the same code. These rules were established after the Reckoning and have been upheld ever since, carried from road to road alongside the lantern that marks each tavern as neutral ground.

Respect the House

The Wayfarer’s Rest is maintained for the benefit of all who pass through. Guests are expected to treat the tavern, its keepers, and fellow travellers with restraint and courtesy, regardless of circumstance or hardship faced upon the road.

No Blood on the Floor

The tavern is a place of rest, not conflict. Old grudges and new disputes must be left outside the door. Violence within the house breaks the Code and is grounds for immediate removal, without exception.

Mind Your Steel

Weapons may be carried on the road, but they are not a language spoken within the tavern. Blades remain sheathed, bows unstrung, and aggressive displays are not tolerated. Secure storage is offered when required.

The Keeper Decides

Each Wayfarer’s Rest is entrusted to its keeper, whose judgement governs the house. In matters of safety, conduct, and fairness, their word is final. This authority is granted not by crown or guild, but by long‑respected custom.
